# Rewrite Patterns

Use this file when the source is grammatically acceptable but still reads like model-generated academic prose.

## Symptom -> preferred response

### Mechanical transitions

Symptoms:
- "First and foremost"
- "It is worth noting that"
- "It should be emphasized that"
- Repeated sentence openings that announce structure rather than advancing the argument

Preferred response:
- Delete the transition if the logic is already clear
- Replace it with a direct claim when a transition is still needed
- Let paragraph order carry coherence instead of constant signposting

### Flat paragraph progression

Symptoms:
- Every sentence has similar weight
- The paragraph never clearly moves from setup to claim to implication
- The writing feels like several correct sentences placed side by side

Preferred response:
- Rebuild the paragraph around one central move
- Move contextual material earlier and keep the strongest claim in the natural emphasis position
- Merge or compress side comments that interrupt progression

### Repeated sentence frames

Symptoms:
- Several consecutive sentences begin with "This study", "This work", "The results", or similar frames
- Clause structure repeats with little variation

Preferred response:
- Vary the grammatical subject when that improves flow
- Combine adjacent sentences that make one point
- Replace repetitive announcements with direct content

### Abstract noun stacking

Symptoms:
- Dense use of words such as "framework", "capability", "effectiveness", "methodology", or "robustness" without enough concrete content
- The prose sounds formal but lands weakly

Preferred response:
- Convert abstractions into clearer verbs or more specific claims
- Keep domain terms that carry real technical meaning
- Remove nominalized filler that exists only to sound academic

### Synonymous repetition

Symptoms:
- One idea is restated in multiple near-equivalent ways
- A sentence repeats what the previous sentence already established

Preferred response:
- Keep the strongest formulation
- Delete the echo unless the repetition adds a necessary qualification
- Avoid "rephrase churn" that creates length without new information

### Over-explanation

Symptoms:
- Obvious field assumptions are spelled out at full length
- Logical bridges are made explicit even when expert readers do not need them
- The text sounds like it is explaining the paper to a novice reader

Preferred response:
- Trust the intended academic audience
- Keep only the information required for precision, interpretation, or argument flow
- Remove sentences that merely restate what competent readers will already infer

### Empty evaluative language

Symptoms:
- Claims that something is "effective", "powerful", "promising", or "significant" without nearby substance
- Self-promotional tone in methods or results

Preferred response:
- Replace the evaluation with the concrete observation that justifies it
- Delete the evaluation if the evidence already speaks for itself
- Preserve necessary caution and scope

### Uniform rhythm

Symptoms:
- Similar sentence length across the paragraph
- Each sentence resolves with the same cadence
- The paragraph has no variation in pressure or pace

Preferred response:
- Mix shorter and longer sentences when natural
- Use sentence splitting or merging to restore rhythm
- Avoid decorative variation that changes the scholarly tone

### Local correctness, global unnaturalness

Symptoms:
- Each sentence is acceptable alone
- The full paragraph still feels unlike a real researcher's prose

Preferred response:
- Edit at paragraph level, not sentence level
- Re-check how each sentence hands off to the next
- Make sure the paragraph has a discernible argumentative shape

## When to preserve the original closely

Keep the source close to its original form when:
- The paragraph already has natural progression
- The wording is specific and technically controlled
- The only possible edits would be cosmetic
- A heavier rewrite would risk flattening the author's voice or intent
