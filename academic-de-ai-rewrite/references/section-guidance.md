# Section Guidance

Use this file when section type affects pacing, emphasis, or acceptable density.

## Introduction

Aim for a clean move from context to gap to contribution.

Prefer:
- compact field setup
- a visible research gap or unresolved limitation
- direct presentation of what the paper addresses

Avoid:
- grand, generic framing that could fit any paper
- repeated importance claims before the specific problem appears
- overly explicit meta-signposting

## Methods

Aim for operational clarity and tight sequencing.

Prefer:
- concrete descriptions of what is done
- stable terminology
- concise explanations of design choices and assumptions

Avoid:
- self-congratulatory adjectives
- vague claims of effectiveness without operational detail
- repetition between method description and method justification

## Results

Aim for evidence-first prose.

Prefer:
- observable findings
- explicit comparison points when they matter
- restrained wording around performance or improvement

Avoid:
- overselling
- repeating the same result in slightly different words
- loading interpretation into every result sentence

## Discussion

Aim for interpretation with control.

Prefer:
- clear linkage between findings and implications
- explicit scope conditions when needed
- concise acknowledgment of tradeoffs or limitations

Avoid:
- broad claims not supported by the reported results
- repeating the results section with only lighter paraphrase
- turning every implication into a general manifesto

## Mixed or unknown section type

If the section type is unclear, infer the paragraph function from what it is doing:
- setting context
- describing an approach
- reporting an observation
- interpreting an observation

Then apply the closest section guidance above.
